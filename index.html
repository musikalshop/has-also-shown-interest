    Mohammed bin Abdulrahman bin Jassim Al-Thani: “Qatar’s sovereign wealth fund has also shown interest in investing in Thai stocks…”

    February 18, 2020February 18, 2020| Nathaniel NakayamaNathaniel Nakayama| 0 Comment

    The private sector is now being urged to work with Qatar as part of its Look East policy, a nation exploring business partnerships with Thai allies on energy, infrastructure, public health, hospitality, and trade.

    Bangkok’s Minister of Foreign Affairs Somkid Jatusripitak spoke with Sheik Mohammed bin Abdulrahman bin Jassim Al-Thani, Qatar’s Deputy Prime Minister and, and discussed how both countries intend to work together on business and investment in energy, infrastructure, and the Eastern Economic Corridors.

    “Qatar’s sovereign wealth fund has also shown interest in investing in Thai stocks, while the Thai government has proposed that Qatar ramp up its investment in Thailand as the gateway to Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, and Vietnam,” said Thai Deputy Prime Minister.

    Qatar has raised $338 billion in assets around the globe enabling its sovereign wealth fund to become the 14th largest in the world. Prime Minister Prayut Chan-o-cha has also given the Qatari foreign minister a call.

    Mr. Somkid aims to head the Investment Board, Economic Ministers, and members of the industry at a visit to Qatar sometime this year. Qatar is Thailand’s third-biggest trade partner in the Middle East and Thailand’s 24th largest trading partner on the world market.

    Bilateral trade in 2018 amounts to $3.91 billion, up 38.6 percent from last year. Exports to Thailand totaled $379.1 million, with $3.52 billion worth of imports. Qatar’s investment in Thailand amounted to $4.32 million in 2018, while Thai investment in Qatar amounted to $609 million in 2018.

    Qatar is the primary energy source for Thailand, which gets 7 percent of its total energy imported from Qatar. In 2018, Thailand’s Qatari visitors numbered 35,838 and were the most searched after health services. Qatar is eager to build branches at Thai hospitals in Qatar, as the nation is seeking to become a Mideast Medical Centre.
